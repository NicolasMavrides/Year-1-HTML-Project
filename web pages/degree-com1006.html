    My Degree/COM1006

    COM1006 (Devices and Networks) is a module which covers material on Computer Arithmetic and Architecture and how computers can be combined to give more complex networks. The module has 2 lectures per week from which the theoretical content is taught, and 1 lab session every Friday during which exercise sheets are covered. COM1006 exercise sheets typically include examples on digital logic calculations (eg. binary and hexadecimal numbers) and the construction of devices or networks from foundamental parts (known as "gates") in a simulator called Logisim (pictured).
